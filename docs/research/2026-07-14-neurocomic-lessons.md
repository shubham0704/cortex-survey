# Neurocomic — what it teaches, and what to steal

*2026-07-14*

## Question
The reference the project started from is *Neurocomic* (Roš & Farinella). What does
it teach, and which of its illustrations are better than the heart demo's treatment
for a brain specifically?

## What we found
The lesson is a **stance**, not a fact. The heart demo (and the rock template it
came from) treat the organ as a dead specimen scanned from outside — a "cadaveric
tissue survey." Neurocomic does the opposite: it shrinks you down and drops you
*inside*, rendering the brain as a world — "neuron forests, memory caves, castles of
deception, giant squid, guitar-playing sea slugs." You learn by wandering it.

That inside-out move is specific to the brain: it's the organ that does the
perceiving, so surveying it from outside like a kidney is a category error.

Metaphor → concept:
- **Forest of neurons** (Cajal's ink trees) → the neuron doctrine + plasticity.
- **Giant squid / sea of synapses** → synaptic transmission.
- **Guitar-playing sea slugs** (Aplysia) → Kandel; learning lives at the synapse.
- **Memory caves** (hippocampus) → encoding / retrieval.
- **Castles of deception** → perception is constructed.
- **Cajal vs Golgi as characters** → Cajal used Golgi's own stain to prove neurons
  are discrete cells, not a continuous net.

The strongest single image is the **forest of neurons**, rooted in Santiago Ramón y
Cajal's real pen-and-ink histology (the "butterflies of the soul"). A photoreal
render can't touch it: it has interiority, and it's the founding art of the field.

## Implication for the build
Stop out-scanning the heart. Turn the scan into a **descent through scales** ending
in a Cajal forest and a firing synapse — the one thing the heart structurally can't
have. The macro-optics window is the natural portal for it.

## Still open
- Which level does the ink / plate aesthetic live at — the whole brain, or only the
  forest?
